What are Genomic Islands?

Genomic islands are mobile DNA segments in bacterial and archaeal genomes acquired through horizontal gene transfer. These regions, typically 10-200 kb in size, carry genes for antibiotic resistance, virulence, metabolism, and environmental adaptationâ€”making them key drivers of prokaryotic evolution and genome plasticity.

Advanced Detection with SeqWord Genomic Island Sniffer

SWIGS automatically searches genomic DNA sequences for loci enriched with horizontally transferred elements, fitness genes, giant genes, or ribosomal RNA/protein genes using oligonucleotide signatures. This signature-based approach surpasses traditional methods that rely on GC content, codon usage, and phylogenetic analysis, providing enhanced sensitivity for detecting horizontally transferred regions across diverse prokaryotic genomes.

Streamlined Analysis Platform: GeNOMic Island

This platform transforms complex SWIGS output files into intuitive, interactive dashboards. Researchers can efficiently explore, compare, and organize genomic island predictions across multiple genomes, enabling rapid insights into horizontal gene transfer patterns and genomic island distribution.
